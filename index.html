Nirmal Surange

I am a PhD researcher at the Language Technologies Research Center (LTRC), IIIT-Hyderabad, specializing in Natural Language Processing (NLP). My research focuses on low-resource NLP and graph-based representations of language, with an emphasis on building interpretable and linguistically grounded models for Indian languages such as Hindi, Marathi, and Telugu.

A central thread of my work involves developing semantically rich, task-specific datasets to support robust evaluation and model design in low-resource scenarios. My contributions span tasks such as text generation, semantic textual relatedness, and emotion analysis.

In parallel with my research, I actively mentor junior researchers and contribute to open, collaborative efforts in NLP. This includes the development of multilingual resources like BRIGHTER, an emotion-annotated corpus across 27 languages, and SemRel, a benchmark for semantic textual relatedness. Through these initiatives, I aim to advance inclusive, accessible NLP technologies, where structured semantic representations can significantly enhance performance in multilingual and under-resourced settings.
